## Why This Thread

This is the right first case study because it concentrates nearly every hard problem the project needs to solve:

- city council decisions
- ordinance amendments
- federal litigation
- city attorney / legal-constraint questions
- county-city coordination
- grants and contracts
- nonprofit and contractor adjacency
- place-based enforcement
- local media framing and recurring quoted actors

It is also narrow enough to be tractable.

## Working Thread Definition

This case study centers on the San Rafael camping-ordinance and Mahon Creek encampment thread, especially:

- `Boyd v. City of San Rafael`
- the City’s 2023 and 2024 camping-ordinance changes
- the post-`Grants Pass` legal and political repositioning
- the sanctioned camping area at Mahon Creek Path
- associated contracts, grants, service-provider roles, and public messaging

## Initial Official Timeline

### July 2023

The City Council passed new camping regulations for public property.

Source:

- City news release summarizing later litigation history: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/news-release-federal-judge-dismisses-lawsuit-limiting-city-regulation-of-camping-on-public-property/

### August 2023 to October 2023

Plaintiffs filed `Boyd v. City of San Rafael`. A TRO and then a preliminary injunction limited enforcement of the ordinance.

Source:

- same city news release: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/news-release-federal-judge-dismisses-lawsuit-limiting-city-regulation-of-camping-on-public-property/

### December 2023

The City published an implementation approach that:

- prohibited camping on Lindaro Street
- prohibited new camping along Mahon Creek Path, Andersen Drive, and Francisco Blvd W
- involved nonprofit partners in needs assessment and outreach
- referenced gift cards, free food, and voluntary-move incentives

Source:

- Camping Ordinance Implementation Approach & Plan: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/camping-ordinance-implementation-approach-plan/

### May 6, 2024

The City Council passed `Ordinance 2035`, introducing updated camping regulations that later became effective June 5, 2024.

Source:

- Homelessness Update: June 2024: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/homelessness-update-june-2024-2/

### June 28, 2024

San Rafael issued a statement on the Supreme Court’s `Grants Pass` decision, saying it was reviewing the ruling’s effect on local enforcement.

Source:

- City statement: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/city-of-san-rafael-statement-on-grants-pass-decision/

### August 7, 2024

Judge Edward Chen granted the City’s motion to dismiss and dissolved the preliminary injunction in `Boyd`.

Source:

- City news release: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/news-release-federal-judge-dismisses-lawsuit-limiting-city-regulation-of-camping-on-public-property/

### August 19, 2024

San Rafael City Council heard a public hearing covering:

- camping ordinance amendments
- informational report on homelessness
- sanctioned camping program
- homelessness program contracts
- appropriation of grant funds

Source:

- Meeting page: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/meetings/city-council-august-19-2024/
- Staff report landing page: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/documents/august-19-2024-staff-report-camping-ordinance-amendments-report-on-homelessness/

### September 2, 2024

The City published a page explaining that `Grants Pass` did not itself provide immediate relief because the local suit was based on state-created-danger and ADA claims, not Eighth Amendment claims.

Source:

- City explainer: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/supreme-courts-grants-pass-ruling-its-effect-on-san-rafaels-encampment-policy/

### October 4, 2024

The sanctioned camping area at Mahon Creek Path opened.

Source:

- November 21, 2024 update: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/homelessness-news-update-november-21-2024/

### November 2024 to January 2025

The City published ongoing updates about site operations, contractors, participant counts, and working-group activity.

Sources:

- November 21, 2024 update: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/homelessness-news-update-november-21-2024/
- January 24, 2025 update: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/homelessness-news-update-january-24-2025/
- Sanctioned camping area page: https://www.cityofsanrafael.org/sanctioned-camping-area/

## Specific Modeling Challenges This Thread Will Test

### 1. Legal reasoning drift

The City’s public explanation changed over time and distinguishes `Grants Pass` from the `Boyd` theory.

The graph needs to preserve:

- what the City said at each point in time
- what the court did
- what meeting materials recommended

### 2. Place-specific enforcement

The ordinance thread is highly geographic.

The graph needs to attach decisions to:

- Mahon Creek Path
- Andersen Drive
- Lindaro Street
- related adjacent sites

### 3. Program operators and nonprofit partners

The sanctioned-camping program involves county, city, nonprofit, and contractor layers.

The graph needs to represent:

- service partners
- security contractors
- program managers
- funding source
- governing approvals

### 4. Media quote framing

This thread is likely to contain exactly the local-media problem we want to model:

- quoted people presented as residents or neighbors
- recurring advocates or affiliates not fully disclosed in article framing
