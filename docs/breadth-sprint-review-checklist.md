# Controlled Breadth Sprint Adversarial Review Checklist

Use this to attack the widening plan before and during execution.

The point is to challenge scope, sequencing, and graph value, not to reopen abstract ontology debates.

## Scope

- Is the sprint still too broad for one phase?
- Are we keeping the phase centered on the San Rafael civic loop instead of trying to deepen every proven source family at once?
- Is there any county-wide lane still pretending to be core sprint scope when it should be conditional or later-phase?
- Are we treating Form `803` as a maintenance lane instead of a fake equal-volume track?

## Sequence

- Is San Rafael City Council `2019+` still the correct first execution slice?
- Should elected disclosure continuity come before broader city-side campaign expansion?
- Is any county track being pulled forward before the city-side query pack proves real improvement?
- Are we stopping at the query gate before widening again?

## Graph Value

- Does this slice improve join density, or mostly increase raw `Record` / `Filing` counts?
- Does this slice materially improve `Actor`, `Meeting`, `Decision`, or `SeatService` continuity?
- Does this slice make the fixed query pack better, or just bigger?
- Are we widening around actual civic questions or just around adapter convenience?

## Query Pack Discipline

- Are we still using the fixed five-query pack without rewriting it to flatter the current data?
- Does `Q1` span multiple filing families and more than one year?
- Does `Q2` resolve imported disclosures back to existing canonical actors and `SeatService` objects cleanly?
- Does `Q3` produce a real San Rafael council timeline beyond the August 19, 2024 worked example?
- Does `Q4` show recurrence across more than one city election cycle without promoting noisy OCR actors?
- Does `Q5` stay bounded and legible?

## Data Quality

- Is full Form `700` import being resisted until the officeholder continuity layer is stronger?
- Is broad row-level campaign `MoneyFlow` still being kept normalized-only unless it is already QA-backed?
- Are non-city-office campaign rows still being kept out of the core sprint?
- Are new imports increasing `missing_target:Actor` or otherwise polluting the actor layer?
- Are `ValidationCheck` boundaries still strong enough to separate extraction failure from source inconsistency?

## Operational Risk

- Is the current slice spending too much time on brittle adapter quirks instead of graph value?
- Are we distinguishing clearly between "public but gated here" and "not operationally worth it right now"?
- Has a supposedly high-value lane turned out to be mostly metadata volume with weak direct artifacts?
- Should a brittle source be paused instead of dragging the whole sprint?

## Import Scope

- Which new bundles should definitely stay out of graph-v1 during the sprint?
- Is broader campaign-money extraction still normalized-only?
- Should the legal pair remain normalized-only until after the city-side breadth checkpoint?
- Are permits, procurement, criminal, and county-wide lanes still correctly deferred?

## Kill Criteria

- What would tell us the sprint is drifting into another architecture exercise?
- What would tell us the sprint is just creating file volume without graph value?
- What should force a pause and re-plan before opening a county-wide lane?

## End-State Decision

At the end of review, force concrete answers to:

1. What exact tracks are in?
2. What exact tracks are out?
3. What is the execution order?
4. What is the fixed query pack used to judge success?
5. What must be proven before Marin County campaign finance or BOS comes back into scope?
