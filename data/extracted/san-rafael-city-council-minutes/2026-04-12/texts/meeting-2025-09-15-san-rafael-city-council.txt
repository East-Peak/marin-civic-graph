    Mayor Kate called the meeting to order at 6:03 p.m. and invited City Clerk Lindsay Lara to call the
    roll. All members of the City Council were present.

    Mayor Kate provided opening remarks, which included gratitude to City Staff, the recent acts of
    political violence across our nation and a land acknowledgment.

    City Clerk Lindsay Lara informed the community that the in-person meeting would be recorded and
    streamed live to YouTube and through Zoom, and that there would be Spanish language
    interpretation. She noted the two-minute timer for public comment and closed captioning on
    Zoom.

3. City Manager and Councilmember Reports:

   City Manager Cristine Alilovich provided the following announcements and updates:
       • National Preparedness Month Proclamation - Appreciation to our Office of Emergency
         Services, Fire and Police Departments
       • Transit Month and alternative commute options
       • Major Development Projects Map on City website – Interactive map of all development
         projects in neighborhoods across San Rafael
       • Tomorrow, the County Board of Supervisors meeting to consider funding for a potential
         City park at 620 Canal Street
       • Chamber of Commerce to host a Level Up Marin Employment Expo on Thursday,
         September 25 at B Street Community Center, 4-7 p.m.
       • San Rafael Library at the Carnegie Building ribbon-cutting celebration to be held
         Saturday, September 20, 12-5 p.m.
       • Announced upcoming community events:
         o West End Block Party to be held Friday, September 19 and Friday, September 26,
             downtown from F to G, 6-9 p.m.
         o Porchfest to be held Sunday, September 21, in the Gerstle Park neighborhood, 12-5
             p.m.
         o Movies in the Park (The Wild Robot) to be held Friday, September 26, at Gerstle
             Park, 6:45–8:45 p.m.
         o Hops and Vines Stroll (presented by the Business Improvement District) to be held
             Saturday, September 27, downtown from Fourth to Lincoln, 2-6 p.m.
         o Marin Sanitary Service Customer Appreciation Day to be held Sunday, September
             28, at 535 Jacoby Street, 9a.m. to 2 p.m.

   City Councilmember Reports:
       • Vice Mayor Bushey reported on a Marin Transit board meeting, a Central Marin
          Sanitation Agency board meeting, the Marin Presiding Judge Breiner’s Memorial Bench
          in Loch Lomond and a ribbon-cutting for the new playground at Peacock Gap Park.
       • Councilmember Llorens Gulati reported on Marin Transit, the 9/11 remembrance
          ceremony, the Canal Alliance Leadership Academy graduation and announced the
          Coastal Cleanup Day to be held this Saturday, September 20 at Pickleweed at 9 a.m.
       • Councilmember Kertz reported on the City/County Schools Quarterly meeting, the
          Ember Stomp event, the Aegis ribbon-cutting, Contempo’s Annual BBQ, the Age-
          Friendly San Rafael meeting and announced the Marin Senior Fair to be held
          Wednesday, September 17.
       • Councilmember Hill reported on the Aegis ribbon-cutting and the Sun Valley
          Neighborhood Association meeting.
       • Mayor Kate commented that her Report was covered by the councilmembers.

Mayor Kate invited public comment; however, there was none.
