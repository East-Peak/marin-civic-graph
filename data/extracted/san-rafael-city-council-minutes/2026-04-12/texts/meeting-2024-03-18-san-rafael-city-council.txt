CITY MANAGER AND COUNCILMEMBER REPORTS:
(including AB 1234 Reports on Meetings and Conferences Attended at City Expense)
3. City Manager and Councilmember Reports:

   City Manager Cristine Alilovich announced:
       • Police Advisory and Accountability Committee meeting to be held Wednesday, March
         20, 2024.
       • SMART free fares to start Monday April 1, 2024 through June 30, 2025 for passengers
         18 years and under, as well as, 65 years and over.
       • Famous San Rafael Explorer – Petals & Ice Exhibit: The Adventures of Louise A. Boyd at
         Boyd Gate House thru September 2024.

   City Councilmember Reports:
       • Vice Mayor Hill reported on an Economic Development Subcommittee meeting and
          announced Art Walk, Downtown Clean and Green on Earth Day and May Madness on
          May 11th. Also, he visited Glenwood Elementary School.
       • Councilmember Kertz visited Glenwood Elementary School and San Pedro Elementary
          School. Also, she attended the Marin Center for Independent Living Open House for
          Caregivers.
       • Councilmember Bushey reported on a Central Marin Sanitation Agency Board meeting,
          two meetings associated with the Point San Pedro Road Coalition, a Loch Lomond
          Oversight Committee meeting, and the Public Service Award Selection Committee
          meeting.
       • Councilmember Llorens Gulati reported on an East San Rafael Working Group meeting
          and a Bicycle and Pedestrian Advisory Committee (BPAC) meeting. Also, she visited
          Bahia Vista School and San Pedro Elementary School.
       • Mayor Kate reported on her Mayoral Fellowship through the Mayors’ Institute on City
          Design with the Harvard School of Design, the San Rafael Employee of the Quarter (Q3)
          winner, the Transportation Authority of Marin Safe Routes to Schools, the Giving Marin
          Independent Journal Awards, the Economic Development Subcommittee, a Voces del
          Canal and Flock Safety event and the 90th birthday party by the Age-Friendly community
          group.

Mayor Kate invited public comment; however, there was none.
