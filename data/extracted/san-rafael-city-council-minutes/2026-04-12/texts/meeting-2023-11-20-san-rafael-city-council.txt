   Mayor Kate called the meeting to order at 7:00 p.m. and invited Deputy City Clerk Brenna Nurmi
   to call the roll. All members of the City Council were present, except for Councilmember Hill.

   City Attorney Rob Epstein announced there was no reportable action in the Closed Session held
   prior to the regular meeting.

   Mayor Kate provided opening remarks, which included expressing gratitude to City staff and first
   responders, the volunteers who serve on the Boards, Commissions & Committees and the
   residents, as well as, a land acknowledgment.

   Deputy City Clerk Brenna Nurmi informed the community that the in-person meeting would also
   be recorded and streamed live to YouTube and through Zoom. She noted the two-minute timer
   for public comment and closed captioning on Zoom.

OPEN TIME FOR PUBLIC EXPRESSION
  • Salamah Locks, Commission on Aging addressed the City Council regarding the
     Commission’s new meeting location, day and time. She announced the next meeting to be
     held Monday, December 11 at 1:30 pm at 1600 Los Gamos Drive, Third Floor. The Topic
     is Changing the Affordable Housing Landscape in the Bay Area - How a New Bond
     Measure Can Help Older Adults. Also, she noted some ACLU Know Your Rights
     information and had ideas for the Police Advisory and Accountability Committee (PAAC).
  • Al Vetere addressed the City Council regarding the pipes along D Street, a metal pipe
     attack over a bicycle, the fast program and an armed robbery in Tiburon.
  • Joe Mulligan, Walk/Bike San Rafael addressed the City Council regarding the Bicycle &
     Pedestrian Advisory Committee (BPAC).
