    Mayor Kate called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. and invited City Clerk Lindsay Lara to call the
    roll. All members of the City Council were present, except for Councilmember Hill.

    Mayor Kate provided opening remarks which included gratitude to City Staff, especially the first
    responders and Department of Public Works for keeping the community safe, guidance on how to
    report an issue on the city’s website and a land acknowledgment.

    City Clerk Lindsay Lara announced there would be Spanish language interpretation for Agenda Item
    6.a and Open Time for Public Expression. She informed the community that the in-person meeting
    would also be recorded and streamed live to YouTube and through Zoom. She noted the two-
    minute timer for public comment and closed captioning on Zoom.

3. City Manager and Councilmember Reports:

   City Manager Cristine Alilovich reported on:
      • Wildfire Preparedness meetings to be held on Thursday, February 6, in Council
         Chambers (and via Zoom), at 6 p.m. and on Tuesday, February 11, a virtual-only meeting
         at noon
      • Park & Recreation Commission meeting to be held on Thursday, February 13, at 6 p.m.
         regarding the Terra Linda Park and Community Center enhancement plan
      • Proposed Residential Parking Permit Program in East San Rafael and Bret Harte
         Neighborhood Update
      • Boards, Commissions and Committees vacancies on the Pickleweed Advisory
         Committee, Planning Commission and Voter Approved Tax Oversight Committee
      • Free Winter Show (with over 75 Bay Area artists) to be held at Falkirk Cultural Center
         Tuesday through Friday, 1-4:30 p.m. and Saturday from 10-2 p.m., through February 21

   City Councilmember Reports:
       • Vice Mayor Bushey reported on a Marin County Council of Mayors and Councilmembers
          (MCCMC) meeting (where they welcomed Councilmember Llorens Gulati to join the
          Marin Transit Board), as well as, a San Rafael Library Foundation meeting regarding
          Measure P. She expressed appreciation to the Department of Public Works for placing
          a generator in Peacock Gap to generate the power necessary to pump out the water
          storage.
       • Councilmember Kertz reported on Marin Wildfire Prevention Authority meetings, a
          MCCMC meeting, a City/County Schools meeting and a Cal Cities Policy Committee for
          Community Services meeting.
       • Councilmember Llorens Gulati reported on 2025 Marin Sea Level Rise BayWAVE
          meetings, a Cal Cities Environmental Quality Policy Committee meeting, the first
          community meeting on the Proposed Residential Parking Permit Program, the MCCMC
          meeting (where she joined the Board for Marin Transit) and a Karma Club visit in Terra
          Linda.
       • Mayor Kate reported on a Transportation Authority of Marin (TAM) meeting (and was
          appointed as Vice Chair for TAM), Sonoma Marin Area Rail Transit (SMART) and a Marin
          Gun Safety Collaborative meeting.

Mayor Kate invited public comment.
